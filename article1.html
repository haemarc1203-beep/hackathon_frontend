Introducing the stock market and stock exchanges

How companies use their profits

When a business makes money, it generally has two main options for what to do with those profits:

  • Pay dividends: share profits directly with shareholders.
  • Reinvest profits: use profits to reinvest or improve operations, develop new products, or hire more employees.

Different companies prioritize these options differently, which leads to different types of stocks.

Types of stocks

  • Income stocks: companies that regularly pay dividends; often chosen by investors who want steady income.
  • Growth stocks: companies that reinvest most profits back into the business; investors aim for long-term growth.

What is a stock exchange?

A stock exchange is a marketplace where investors can buy and sell shares. Exchanges provide rules, transparency, and pricing information.
